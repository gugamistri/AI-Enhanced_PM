# Product Requirements Document (PRD)

## Título do Projeto
AI-Powered Product Management: From Pilot to Production

## Objetivo
Desenvolver um livro estratégico e prático para CEOs, líderes de produto e gestores de engenharia que buscam liderar transformações com IA em mercados tradicionais, cobrindo desde a concepção até a implementação e escalabilidade.

## Público-Alvo
- CEOs
- Líderes de Produto
- Gestores de Engenharia
- Executivos de empresas tradicionais em transformação digital

## Diferenciais do Livro
- Estrutura completa e sistemática para transformação com IA
- Foco em mercados tradicionais e desafios reais
- Ferramentas, frameworks e business cases prontos para uso
- Metodologia validada e exemplos de casos reais

## Estrutura Geral do Livro
- 4 Partes principais (Fundamentos, Metodologia, Estudos de Caso, Liderança)
- 19 capítulos cobrindo desde estratégia até execução e liderança
- Apêndices com ferramentas, templates e recursos práticos
- Capítulos já criados e estruturados conforme ebook_structure.md

## Entregáveis
- Livro completo com 19 capítulos
- Apêndices com frameworks, templates e guias
- Ferramentas de avaliação, planejamento e execução para o leitor
- Cada capitulo vai ser escrito em um arquivo separado no formato: chapter_XX.md. Ex. chapter_1.md, chapter_10.md


## Writing Style
- Professional business language with optimistic, engaging tone
- Clear, immediately actionable content that drives reader completion
- Use real-world cases and concrete examples with verified sources
- Include practical illustrations and frameworks readers can implement
- Balance strategic insights with tactical implementation guidance
- Maintain reader engagement through compelling storytelling and proven results
- Avoid academic jargon while maintaining executive-level authority
- Create compelling chapter transitions that build anticipation and momentum
- Write with confidence and expertise expected by senior product leaders

## Critérios de Sucesso
- Estrutura do livro validada e alinhada ao público-alvo
- Capítulos revisados e refinados conforme plano
- Conteúdo prático, aplicável e diferenciado

**Required Reference Types:**
1. **McKinsey Research Reports** - Use actual McKinsey Global Institute reports on AI adoption, productivity, and transformation (State of AI, Economic Impact of AI, etc.)
2. **Academic Research** - Cite peer-reviewed studies from Harvard Business Review, MIT Sloan Management Review, Stanford research, Journal of Product Innovation Management
3. **Industry Studies** - Reference reports from Gartner, Forrester, PwC, Deloitte, BCG on AI transformation and product management
4. **Expert Quotes** - Include real statements from recognized thought leaders (Marty Cagan, John Maeda, Reid Hoffman, etc.)
5. **Company Case Studies** - Reference publicly available transformation stories from documented companies
6. **Government/Regulatory Sources** - Use data from official sources, SEC filings, regulatory reports where applicable

**Reference Format & Standards:**
- In-text citations: "According to McKinsey's 2024 State of AI Report, 78% of organizations have adopted AI in at least one business function (McKinsey Global Institute, 2024)..."
- Include full citations at chapter end with proper bibliographic format
- Use real publication dates, authors, and verifiable sources only
- Cross-reference multiple sources for major statistical claims
- Ensure all McKinsey references are from actual published reports
- Validate expert quotes are real and properly attributed

**Source Priority Ranking:**
1. **Tier 1:** McKinsey, BCG, Bain research reports and case studies
2. **Tier 2:** Harvard Business Review, MIT research, peer-reviewed academic journals
3. **Tier 3:** Gartner, Forrester, PwC industry analyst reports
4. **Tier 4:** Company annual reports, SEC filings, public transformation case studies
5. **Tier 5:** Reputable business media (Wall Street Journal, Financial Times, etc.)

**Validation Standards:**
- Never fabricate statistics, research findings, or case study results
- Use only publicly available, verifiable sources
- Verify all statistics are current and accurately represented
- Include proper disclaimers when using anonymized examples
- Ensure claims align with what sources actually state, not interpretations
